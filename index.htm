Something About Myself

I was born on October 21st, 1981, in Zagreb, Croatia. I currently live in Dugo Selo, a small town 20 km east of Zagreb. I was a student of the 2nd year of Technical School Ruđer Bošković.

My interests include programming, playing good games like Doom, Warcraft II, Diablo, Quake II, and Dota 2. I spend most of my time programming in Borland Delphi and Lazarus because they are powerful programming tools, and I love to use them. I also have programming experience in C/C++ and do some DOS programs in that language.

When I'm not at the computer, I like to ride bicycles, hang out with friends, listen to good music (check out the MP3 page), play basketball, watch movies, and do all other things that an average young man likes to do.

Delphi & Lazarus Components Made by Me in High school

Component Name Description
TDotMatrix (6.3 KB) This is a component that looks like a dot matrix. Properties Caption, ForeColor, BackColor, and Spacing control this component. It does not use any bitmap resources. Read README.TXT in the archive for more info. Demo included. Freeware with source, Delphi 1, 2, 3, and C++ Builder compatible. Also compatible with Lazarus.
TLedNumber (3 KB) This component is based on TDotMatrix; characters look like LED numbers on hand calculators. This component additionally includes LineWidth and Spacing properties that define the width of characters and spacing between them. Read README.TXT in the archive for more info. Freeware with source, Delphi 1, 2, 3, and C++ Builder compatible. Also compatible with Lazarus.
TFlatButton (6.9 KB) Delphi Microsoft Office 97 Style SpeedButton. This component is intended for Delphi 2.0 users. Freeware with source, Delphi 1, 2, 3, and C++ Builder compatible. Also compatible with Lazarus.

I have designed many other components that I use in my applications. They are still not available for download, and they may be released someday. You can email me for more details about my work.
